Your Map for Today 🗺️

BIG IDEA: people haven't always believed the Sun is at the centre of the solar system — it took centuries of careful observation to change everyone's mind.
🎯 By the end you can: describe the old Earth-centred idea and the newer Sun-centred idea, explain why ancient people believed what they did, and tell which model a description is talking about.

Quick Recall from Unit 7 🧠

You already learned: the Sun is a star at the centre of our solar system. Planets, including Earth, orbit the Sun. The Moon orbits Earth, not the Sun directly.
Today we go further: that Sun-centred picture is actually called the heliocentric model — and it wasn't always what people believed. For most of human history, nearly everyone believed the exact opposite: that Earth stood still at the centre, and everything else circled around US.

Why learn this? 🤔

This is one of the biggest changes of mind in the whole history of science. Understanding WHY people believed the old idea (it made sense from what they could see with their own eyes) and WHY they eventually changed their minds (new evidence, especially from telescopes) teaches you something bigger than astronomy — it teaches you how good evidence can overturn even a belief almost everyone holds.
Real life: scientists still update their beliefs today when strong new evidence comes in — that's not a weakness of science, it's exactly how science is supposed to work.

The Old Idea: Earth at the Centre 🌍

GEOCENTRIC MODEL: Earth stays still at the centre. The Sun, Moon, planets, and stars all circle around EARTH.
In this old model, Earth doesn't move at all — the Sun, Moon, and planets are pictured circling around it, one after another.
Why people believed this: stand still outside and watch the sky — the Sun appears to rise, cross the sky, and set. The stars appear to wheel overhead at night. You don't FEEL the Earth moving at all under your feet. From this everyday experience alone, "Earth is still, and everything else moves around us" was a completely reasonable conclusion — for thousands of years, this was the accepted scientific model, not a silly mistake.

The New Idea: Sun at the Centre ☀️

HELIOCENTRIC MODEL: the Sun stays still at the centre. Earth and the other planets all orbit AROUND the Sun.
In this model, the Sun stays put, and Earth is just one of several planets travelling around it — matching what you already learned in Unit 7.
Who changed the picture: a scientist named Copernicus proposed this Sun-centred model. It explained the same everyday sky observations just as well as the old model — so for a long time, there was no way to prove which one was actually correct. That changed once a new tool came along: the telescope.

Which Model Is It Describing? 🌳

Does the description say Earth stays still, with the Sun and planets circling AROUND Earth?
YES
GEOCENTRIC model (the old, Earth-centred idea)
NO — the Sun stays still, Earth and planets circle AROUND the Sun
HELIOCENTRIC model (the modern, Sun-centred idea — the one that's actually correct)
How to use this: find the word "centre" in the question and ask — is EARTH sitting still in the centre, or is the SUN sitting still in the centre? Whichever object is described as NOT moving, staying fixed in the middle, tells you which model is being described.
