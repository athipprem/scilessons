Quick Fix: The Iron Question 🩸

Iron is a mineral that helps your blood carry oxygen around your body. Too little iron means your blood can't deliver oxygen as well — and that makes you feel tired and look pale.

Twice now, when a question described someone with symptoms that point to low iron, the answer picked was Calcium instead. Let's slow down and separate two different skills: (1) recognizing the SYMPTOMS of low iron, and (2) understanding WHY those symptoms happen.

The mineral to remember: Iron. The job: helping blood carry oxygen. The symptoms when you don't have enough: feeling unusually tired, and looking pale.

Quick Check 1: A student feels extremely tired and looks unusually pale for several weeks, even though she sleeps enough and eats plenty of food overall. A doctor suggests she may be low on a specific mineral and recommends more red meat and leafy greens. Which mineral is this most likely about?

✔ Correct — tiredness and paleness, treated with iron-rich foods like red meat and leafy greens, point to low iron.

Quick Recall from Unit 17 🔁

🔁 Remember what a seed needs to germinate? A seed needs exactly THREE things to start sprouting: WATER, the right WARMTH, and AIR. It does NOT need soil or light to germinate — its own stored food is enough at first.

That rule is correct — but today you'll learn exactly WHY warmth matters and WHY soil doesn't, so this never gets muddled again. Then you'll learn what happens the moment a seed finishes sprouting.

Why learn this? 🤔

Farmers decide exactly when to plant based on soil warmth, not soil richness — planting too early in cold soil wastes seeds even in the best soil available. Scientists who store seeds in seed banks (vaults that protect seeds for the future) keep them cold and dry on purpose, to stop them from germinating too soon.
Real life: gardeners germinate seeds indoors on a warm windowsill using nothing but a damp paper towel — no soil at all — then move the sprouted seedling into soil only once it needs to keep growing.

The Real Reasons Behind the Rule 🔍

WARMTH matters because every seed is packed with tiny helper substances that only work properly at the right temperature. SOIL isn't needed yet because the seed already carries its own food supply.
🌡️
Why warmth?
Inside every seed are tiny helper substances that break down its stored food into usable energy — but they only work at the right temperature. Too cold, and they barely work at all; too hot, and they stop working properly. That's why warmth (not soil) decides whether a seed wakes up.
🎒
Why not soil?
A seed is like a tiny backpack, already stocked with a food supply built in when it formed. That stored food powers the very first sprouting, so the seed doesn't need to draw any nutrients from soil yet.
💧
Why water?
Water softens the tough seed coat and helps activate those same tiny helper substances — without it, they simply can't start working, no matter how warm it is.
Key idea: soil is rich in nutrients, but a germinating seed isn't using outside nutrients yet — it's living off its own built-in food supply. Warmth isn't about "cosy soil," it's about the temperature those tiny helper substances need to work. This is exactly why a bean can sprout in a wet paper towel with no soil at all, as long as it's warm enough.

What Happens Right After Germination? 🌿

The moment a seed finishes sprouting and becomes a SEEDLING, its built-in food supply starts running out — and that's exactly when soil and light finally become essential.
🌱 Germinating seed
Needs: water, warmth, air.
Does NOT need: soil, light.
Living off its own stored food.
🌿 Growing seedling
Needs: water, warmth, air, PLUS soil (for nutrients & anchoring roots) AND light (for photosynthesis, from Unit 8).
Its own stored food is used up.
Key idea: "soil and light aren't needed" is only true for the germination STAGE, for a short window right at the very start. As soon as the seedling's own food store runs out, it must start making its own food through photosynthesis (needs light) and drawing water and nutrients through its roots (needs soil) — otherwise it cannot keep growing.

Which Stage, Which Needs? 🌳

Has the seed already sprouted and grown its first tiny leaves?
NO — still germinating
Needs water, warmth, air only — soil and light not required yet
YES — now a seedling
Needs water, warmth, air, PLUS soil and light — its own food store is running out
How to use this: always check which STAGE a question is describing first — a bare, ungerminated seed sitting in a packet, or a plant that has already sprouted leaves — before deciding whether soil and light matter.
