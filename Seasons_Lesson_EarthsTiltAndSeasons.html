Quick Recall from Unit 7 🧠

You already learned: Earth SPINS on its axis once every 24 hours (ROTATION — gives us day and night), and Earth ORBITS the Sun once every year (REVOLUTION). You also learned that as Earth orbits, your night side points toward different stars at different times of year.
Today we go further: that same axis Earth spins on isn't standing perfectly straight up and down — it's TILTED. That tilt is the single most important fact behind everything you're about to learn about the seasons.

Why learn this? 🤔

Seasons affect almost everything in daily life — what clothes you pack, when farmers plant and harvest crops, when school holidays fall, even which sports are played when. Most people can tell you WHAT season it is, but very few can correctly explain WHY it happens. Understanding the real cause (not the popular myth) is exactly the kind of "think like a scientist" reasoning ASMOPSS rewards.
Real life: airlines, farmers, and clothing stores all plan months ahead based on which hemisphere's summer or winter is coming — get the reasoning backwards, and those plans fail.

Earth's Tilted Axis 📐

Earth's axis is tilted about 23.5° — and it always points in the SAME direction in space, all year round, as Earth orbits the Sun.
The tilt line on Earth never changes direction as it orbits — but because Earth moves around the Sun, that FIXED tilt sometimes points a hemisphere toward the Sun, and six months later, points that same hemisphere away.
Key idea: nothing about the tilt itself changes during the year — what changes is WHERE Earth is in its orbit, which determines whether a hemisphere's tilt happens to point toward or away from the Sun at that time.

Busting the Big Myth ❌

🚫 Common myth: "Earth has seasons because it moves closer to the Sun in summer and farther away in winter." This is FALSE — and the real facts prove it backwards! Earth is actually slightly CLOSEST to the Sun in early January (Northern Hemisphere winter) and FARTHEST from the Sun in early July (Northern Hemisphere summer).
The TRUE cause: a tilted hemisphere facing the Sun gets more direct, concentrated sunlight (and longer days) — a tilted hemisphere facing away gets more slanted, spread-out sunlight (and shorter days).
Same amount of sunlight, two different angles: hitting straight-on packs the energy into a small patch (concentrated warmth); hitting at a slant spreads that same energy over a much bigger patch (weaker warmth per spot). A tilted-toward hemisphere gets the direct, concentrated version.
The two-part rule: a hemisphere tilted TOWARD the Sun gets (1) more direct, concentrated sunlight, AND (2) longer daylight hours — both add up to summer. A hemisphere tilted AWAY gets slanted, spread-out sunlight and shorter days — both add up to winter.

Which Season Is It? 🌳

At this point in Earth's orbit, is THIS hemisphere tilted toward the Sun, or away from it?

How to use this: ignore Earth's distance from the Sun entirely — it's a myth, not a clue. The only question that matters is which way THIS hemisphere's tilt is currently pointing.
