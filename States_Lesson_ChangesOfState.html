Quick Recall from Unit 4 🦠

🔁 Remember mould? Warmth sped mould growth up, and cold (like a fridge) slowed it down — temperature changed HOW FAST something happened.

Today, temperature does something even bigger: it changes WHAT STATE matter is in. Heat an ice cube and it doesn't just melt faster or slower — it turns from solid to liquid completely. Same idea (temperature matters!) applied to a new kind of change.

Mini self-check: in Unit 4, did WARMTH make mould grow (1) faster, or (2) slower? (Answer: faster — keep "temperature changes things" in mind for today.)

Why learn this? 🤔

Ice cubes melting in a drink, puddles drying up after rain, a cold glass "sweating" on a hot day, water freezing into ice cubes in the freezer — these are all changes of state, happening around you every single day. ASMOPSS loves asking you to name the correct change and explain whether heating or cooling caused it.
Real life: chefs melt butter and freeze ice cream on purpose. Weather depends on evaporation and condensation (that's how clouds and rain form!). Even your breath "steaming up" a cold window is water vapour condensing back into tiny liquid droplets.

The Four Changes of State 🔄

HEATING pushes matter solid → liquid → gas. COOLING pushes it back gas → liquid → solid.
🔥 HEATING (adding heat)
Melting: solid → liquid
Evaporating: liquid → gas
❄️ COOLING (removing heat)
Freezing: liquid → solid
Condensing: gas → liquid
Key fact: the same substance can go through ALL of these — water can be ice (solid), water (liquid), or steam/water vapour (gas), and change between any of them just by heating or cooling.

Same Matter, No Mass Lost ⚖️

A change of state does NOT create or destroy matter — it just rearranges it. Total mass stays the same (unless something escapes into the air, like evaporation into open air).
Proof by scale: weigh an ice cube in a sealed bag. Let it melt completely, still sealed. Weigh the bag again — the mass is exactly the same, because every bit of the ice is still there, just as liquid water now.
Careful with evaporation: if you leave a puddle OPEN to the air and it evaporates, the puddle's water doesn't vanish either — it becomes water vapour and spreads into the air around it. If you could weigh the room's air before and after, that mass would have gone there instead. Nothing is ever truly lost, even when it looks like it disappeared.

Spot the Change in Real Life 🔍

Practice naming the change: ask "did it get hotter or colder?" and "which two states are involved?"
🧊➡️💧 Ice cube melts in a drink
Heating, solid → liquid = MELTING
💧➡️☁️ A puddle dries up in the sun
Heating, liquid → gas = EVAPORATING
☁️➡️💧 A cold drink glass "sweats"
Cooling, gas → liquid = CONDENSING
💧➡️🧊 Water turns to ice in the freezer
Cooling, liquid → solid = FREEZING
Try it yourself: steam from a hot shower forms droplets on a cold mirror. What change is that? (Cooling, gas → liquid — that's condensing, the same idea as the sweating glass.)

✔ Correct — gas cooling into a liquid is condensing.

✔ Exactly — melting doesn't destroy matter, so the mass stays the same.
✘ Remember: a change of state rearranges matter, it doesn't remove any of it.

4. Which change of state happens when you ADD heat to a liquid until it becomes a gas?

✔ Right — heating a liquid until it becomes gas is evaporating.
