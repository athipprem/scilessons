Why learn this? 🤔

Stir a spoon of sugar into water and it vanishes from sight. Did it stop being matter? Once you understand mass and volume, you can prove the answer with a simple kitchen-scale experiment.
Real life: a big bag of popcorn can weigh less than a small bar of chocolate — big doesn't mean heavy! Bakers, cooks, and scientists all rely on knowing the difference between mass and volume. Your superpower: never get fooled by size again.

Mass vs Volume ⚖️📦

MASS = amount of matter (weight on a scale). VOLUME = amount of space it takes up.
MASS
measured with a scale (grams, kg)
tells you how much "stuff"
VOLUME
measured with a ruler/jug (cm³, mL)
tells you how much space
Key idea: a big object isn't always heavy, and a small object isn't always light. Size (volume) and weight (mass) are two separate measurements.

Does Matter Disappear When It Dissolves? 🥄

NO! Dissolving spreads matter into tiny particles too small to see — it doesn't destroy it.
What really happens: the sugar breaks into particles far too tiny to see. They spread evenly through the water — that's why the water tastes sweet everywhere, not just at the bottom. The sugar is still there, just hidden!
Proof by scale: weigh a COVERED cup with water + a sugar cube. Let it dissolve. Weigh it again — still covered, nothing added or removed. The mass is exactly the SAME as before. If the sugar had truly vanished, the mass would have dropped — but it doesn't.

Think Like a Scientist 🔬

FAIR TEST RULE: change ONE thing, measure ONE thing, keep everything else the SAME.

Imagine testing: "Does crushing sugar make it dissolve faster?" To be fair:

CHANGE
one thing
(lump vs crushed)
MEASURE
one thing
(time to dissolve)
SAME
everything else
(water amount, temperature, stirring)
Why crushing matters: crushing the sugar into crystals gives it far more surface touching the water at once, so it dissolves faster — same total sugar, same total mass, just broken into more pieces.
🧠 Big habit for the mass-scale experiment: keep the cup covered. If water vapour escapes, the mass would drop for a reason that has NOTHING to do with dissolving — that would ruin the fair test.
