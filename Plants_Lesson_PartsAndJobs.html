Why learn this? 🤔

Look at your dinner plate — you are eating plant parts! Knowing which part is which is a real superpower.
🥕 Carrot = root 🥔 Potato = stem (underground!) 🥬 Lettuce = leaf 🥦 Broccoli = flower 🍎 Apple = holds seeds
Your superpower: once you know each part's job, you can look at any plant — or any vegetable — and figure out what it does and why it grows that way. Scientists in the ASMOPSS contest love asking exactly this!

Roots 🥕

Roots do 2 jobs: ANCHOR the plant + DRINK water from the soil.
Job 1 — Anchor: roots spread out and grip the soil so wind and rain can't knock the plant over. (Think of tent pegs!)
Job 2 — Drink: tiny root hairs soak up water and minerals from the soil, like hundreds of little straws.

Stem 🪵

The stem does 2 jobs: HOLD the plant up + CARRY water and food.
Job 1 — Support: the stem stands tall and holds the leaves up to the sunlight, and holds the flowers up for insects.
Job 2 — Transport: inside the stem are tiny tubes (like bundled straws) that carry water UP from the roots and carry food AROUND the plant.

The Tricky Bit: Underground Stems 🕵️

Some STEMS grow UNDER the soil — they look like roots, but they aren't!

Surprise: a potato is not a root — it's a stem that grows underground and stores food! So are ginger, onion, and turmeric.

🥕
Root (carrot)
• thin, pointy, hairy
• NO buds or "eyes"
• just drinks & anchors
🥔
Underground STEM (potato)
• has "eyes" = buds
• buds can grow new shoots
• stores food
🔑 The test: a stem has buds (places new leaves/shoots can sprout). Roots never have buds. Potato eyes = buds → potato is a STEM.
Underground stems to remember: 🥔 potato, 🧅 onion, 🫚 ginger, turmeric. (Carrot, radish, sweet potato = roots.)

Leaves 🍃

The leaf's main job: MAKE FOOD using sunlight. (It's the plant's kitchen.)
Make food: leaves catch sunlight and use it (with water + air) to make sugar — the plant's food. This is called photosynthesis (we'll dig into it later).
Tiny holes: leaves have microscopic holes to let air in and out. Remember these — they matter in Lesson 2!

Flower 🌸

The flower's main job: MAKE SEEDS (so new plants can grow).
Make seeds: flowers attract insects, get pollinated, and then grow seeds. Each seed can become a brand-new plant. That's how plants make babies!
🍅 Fruits (apple, tomato) form around the seeds to protect them and help them spread.
