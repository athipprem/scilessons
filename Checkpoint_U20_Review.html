Quick Recap Cards

🌳 Materials (U12)Rubber comes from tree sap — a PLANT material. Silk is spun by silkworms — an ANIMAL material. The two classic mix-ups! Sort any material by hard/soft, flexible/rigid, and floats/sinks in water AND oil.
💡 Light & Shadows (U13–15)A POINT source (small torch bulb) makes ONE sharp shadow — umbra only. A BROAD source (long fluorescent tube) makes a softer shadow with a fuzzy penumbra fringe around a smaller umbra.
⚖️ Density (U15)Density compares MASS for the SAME volume. An object floats if it's LESS dense than the liquid around it, and sinks if it's MORE dense.
🔊 Sound (U16)Sound travels FASTEST through solids, medium speed through liquids, SLOWEST through gases — because of how close together the particles are.
💨 Air & Nitrogen (U17)Air is roughly 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen. Plants can't use nitrogen gas directly — root bacteria do NITROGEN FIXATION, turning it into a form plant roots CAN use.
🌍 Pollution vs Greenhouse Effect (U17)The greenhouse effect is a NATURAL process that keeps Earth warm enough for life. Pollution (like extra carbon dioxide from burning fuel) makes that natural effect STRONGER, causing extra warming.
🌱 Germination (U17)A seed needs WATER, WARMTH, and AIR to germinate — NOT soil, and NOT light. A resting seed is still alive, just dormant.
🌸 Flower Parts (U17)STIGMA catches pollen. ANTHER makes pollen. OVARY holds the ovules and becomes the fruit. OVULE becomes the seed after fertilization.
⚡ Potential vs Kinetic Energy (U18)POTENTIAL energy = stored energy from position, height, or stillness. KINETIC energy = the energy of MOTION. In a hydroelectric dam: water in the RESERVOIR (potential) flows down the PENSTOCK, spins the TURBINE (kinetic), which powers the GENERATOR.
🔍 Observation vs Inference (cross-unit skill)An OBSERVATION is something you directly SEE, hear, or measure. An INFERENCE is a conclusion you WORK OUT based on that observation — not the same thing!
💧 The Water Cycle (U19)Evaporation → Condensation → Precipitation → Collection, then the whole loop repeats — water is never created or destroyed, only moved and changed in form.

🌊 Priority Refresher — Tide Frequency

The rule: tides are NOT a once-a-day thing. There are roughly TWO high tides and TWO low tides every single day. A high tide is followed by the next HIGH tide about 12 hours later, and by the nearest LOW tide about 6 hours later (roughly halfway in between).

On Unit 19's test, two questions tested this exact idea from different angles, and both were missed — both pointed to the same mix-up: thinking there's only ONE tide cycle a day (one high, one low), instead of TWO of each. Let's lock this in before the checkpoint test.

Quick Check 1: Which statement is TRUE about how tides are spaced out?

✔ Correct — same-type tides (high-to-high, or low-to-low) are roughly 12 hours apart; the opposite type falls roughly halfway between, about 6 hours later.
✘ Same-type tides (high-to-high) are roughly 12 hours apart; the opposite type (low) falls roughly halfway between, about 6 hours later.

Quick Check 2: A tide chart shows a beach had a high tide, then a low tide, then ANOTHER high tide, all within one day. Would you expect one more low tide before that day is over?

✔ Correct — a typical day has roughly two of EACH tide type, so high→low→high naturally continues into one more low.
✘ A typical day has roughly TWO high tides and TWO low tides — high→low→high should be followed by one more low.
