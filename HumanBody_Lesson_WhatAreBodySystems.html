Quick Recall from Unit 8 🌿

🔁 Photosynthesis check: back in Unit 8, you learned that PLANTS take in carbon dioxide and release oxygen during photosynthesis — using sunlight to make their own food.

Your body works the opposite way around! You breathe IN oxygen and breathe OUT carbon dioxide, and instead of making food from sunlight, you eat food and your body breaks it down for energy. Today's lesson is all about the teams of organs inside YOUR body that make this happen.

Why learn this? 🤔

Understanding body systems helps you understand your own health — why you breathe harder when you run, why eating well gives you energy, and why doctors check your pulse and breathing when you're unwell. It's also the foundation for understanding sports science, nutrition, and how medicines work.
Real life: doctors listen to your heart and lungs with a stethoscope to check your circulatory and respiratory systems are working properly. Athletes train their circulatory system so their heart can pump more blood (and oxygen) to their muscles. Nutritionists study the digestive system to recommend healthy eating.

Meet Three Body Systems 🧍

An ORGAN SYSTEM is a group of organs that work together to do ONE big job for the body.
🍽️ Digestive
mouth, stomach, intestines
breaks down food into nutrients your body can use
🫁 Respiratory
nose, windpipe, lungs
brings oxygen INTO the body, removes carbon dioxide
🫀 Circulatory
heart, blood, blood vessels
pumps blood, carrying oxygen & nutrients all around the body
Key idea: each system is made of several different organs working as a team — no single organ could do the whole job alone. The stomach can't digest food without the rest of the digestive system; the heart can't deliver oxygen without the lungs supplying it first.

Teamwork Between Systems 🤝

Body systems don't work alone — they constantly pass things to each other to keep you alive and energised.
🍌 You eat a banana
→
🍽️ Digestive system breaks it into nutrients
→
🫀 Circulatory system carries nutrients in the blood
→
💪 Muscle cells use the nutrients for energy
Key idea: at the very same time, your respiratory system is loading your blood with oxygen with every breath, and your circulatory system delivers that oxygen to the very same muscle cells — nutrients AND oxygen are both needed together to release energy. Three systems, one shared job.

Which System Is Doing This Job? 🔍

Use this checklist to work out which system a described job belongs to.
Is the job mainly about BREAKING DOWN FOOD, or about MOVING things (oxygen, nutrients, blood) AROUND the body?
↓
BREAKING DOWN FOOD
DIGESTIVE system
e.g. chewing, stomach churning food, absorbing nutrients
MOVING things around
Ask: is it about AIR (in/out of lungs) or BLOOD (pumped around the body)?
Air → Respiratory. Blood → Circulatory.
Key idea: "taking a deep breath" is about air moving in and out, so that's respiratory. "Your heart beating faster" is about blood being pumped, so that's circulatory. "Your stomach growling before lunch" is about food being processed, so that's digestive.
