Quick Recall from Unit 7 🌍

🔁 Rotation check: Earth's ROTATION (its daily spin) is what causes day and night — your side of Earth gets sunlight during the day, and is in shadow at night.

Keep that connected to today's topic: photosynthesis needs SUNLIGHT, so it happens during the DAY — the same daylight that Earth's rotation gives us. Two different units, one connected idea: no sunlight reaching a plant means no photosynthesis, just like no sunlight reaching your side of Earth means night.

Why learn this? 🤔

Nearly every bite of food you eat traces back to a plant making food through photosynthesis — even meat comes from animals that ate plants (or ate other animals that did). The oxygen you're breathing right now was very likely released by a plant doing photosynthesis. It's one of the most important processes on the entire planet.
Real life: farmers plan crops around sunlight, water, and soil because they're feeding photosynthesis. Greenhouse growers add extra carbon dioxide and light on purpose to make plants grow faster. Understanding the "recipe" for photosynthesis explains why plants wilt without light or water — they can't cook their own food.

The Photosynthesis Recipe 🌞

Plants combine THREE ingredients using energy from sunlight to make food (glucose) — and release oxygen as a bonus product.
Key idea: sunlight (energy) + water (from roots) + carbon dioxide (from air) → glucose (food, stored/used by the plant) + oxygen (released into the air). This is why we say plants "make their own food" — they don't eat anything solid, they build food from simple ingredients using light energy.

Where It Happens: Chlorophyll & Leaves 🍃

Photosynthesis happens mainly in LEAVES, inside a green substance called CHLOROPHYLL that captures sunlight energy.
Key idea: chlorophyll is GREEN, which is exactly why most leaves look green — it's the substance doing the light-capturing work. Parts of a plant without chlorophyll (like roots, or a potato's underground stem) cannot do photosynthesis themselves; they rely on food made by the green parts and moved to them through the plant's tubes.

Is Photosynthesis Happening Here? 🌳

Use this checklist to decide whether a part of a plant is doing photosynthesis right now.
Does this part contain chlorophyll (is it green) AND is it currently getting sunlight?
↓
YES to both
Photosynthesis CAN happen here right now
e.g. a green leaf in daylight
NO to either
Photosynthesis is NOT happening here right now
e.g. a root underground, or a leaf at night, or a leaf kept in the dark
Key idea: a green leaf outside on a sunny afternoon = photosynthesis happening. That SAME leaf at midnight = no photosynthesis (no sunlight), even though it's still green. A potato underground = no photosynthesis ever (no chlorophyll, no light reaches it) — it just stores food made elsewhere in the plant.
